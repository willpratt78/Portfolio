About Me

Having spent most of my adult life working in kitchens, I feel that it's time to take on a new challenge and learn a new skill set with all the trials and tribulations that have gone on in the world for the last few years. I have always had an interest in computers and software, had my high school not had a culinary program I stumbled upon that's what I was planning on studying and going into as a career. The fact that coding is getting to constantly solve different problems/puzzles and seeing direct results from that, which is also one of the reasons that rock climbing is my biggest hobby, keeps me engaged with the work and excited to take on new challenges. While it may not be apparent at first I think there are a lot of similarities in the structures of kitchens, software development, and the soft skills needed to perform both jobs.

Transferable soft skills from kitchens

  1. You work on your own project as an individual to create a complete product as a team.
  2. Having to find creative solutions to problems quickly when something unexpected happens.
  3. Working with different people with all kinds of backgrounds and potential language barriers.
  4. Constantly adjusting things to meet consumer demand but never really communicating with the consumer directly always through a 3rd party.
  5. Being a self-starter who will show up and get the job done without having someone watch them constantly
  6. Taking in constructive criticism well, and then apply it immediately upon receiving it.
  7. Knowing that the first time you approach something isn't always going to be the desired result and being willing to go back until it's right.
